# Review Decision Quality

Improve the decision process without pretending uncertainty can be eliminated.

## Define the decision

State:

- decision owner and deadline;
- objective and constraints;
- stakeholders and affected users;
- options, including delay, experiment, and status quo;
- what is reversible and what creates lock-in;
- success measures and unacceptable outcomes.

Separate facts, estimates, preferences, and assumptions.

## Evaluate options

Use criteria tied to the outcome. Consider:

- expected user and strategic value;
- evidence strength;
- cost, time, opportunity cost, and maintenance;
- reversibility and option value;
- dependencies and concentration risk;
- security, privacy, safety, and reputational downside;
- learning generated by the option;
- fit with existing capabilities and goals.

Where useful, estimate ranges rather than false-precision point values. Test sensitivity: which assumption flips the recommendation?

## Challenge

Run a pre-mortem on the leading option. Seek disconfirming evidence. Identify dominated options, sunk-cost reasoning, novelty bias, and criteria chosen to justify a favorite.

## Choose the next commitment

For uncertain but reversible choices, prefer a bounded experiment with a decision rule. For irreversible choices, raise the evidence and approval threshold. Record why the decision was reasonable with information available now.

## Output

Provide the decision statement, options, criteria, evidence and assumptions, trade-off matrix, sensitivity, recommendation, confidence, experiment or implementation gate, and review date.
