# Discover High-Leverage Opportunities

Generate from evidence and tensions, not from feature-list clichÃ©s.

## Frame

Capture the user's goals, current capabilities, assets, constraints, recurring frustrations, abandoned ideas, and adjacent projects. Distinguish:

- proven user need;
- inferred need;
- technical possibility;
- speculative bet.

## Open the search space

Generate candidates through several lenses:

- pain removal: repeated friction, waiting, re-entry, manual reconciliation;
- outcome expansion: what valuable job begins just before or after the current product?
- latent capability: data, models, workflows, or interfaces already present but disconnected;
- cross-project fusion: what becomes possible only by combining two existing systems?
- trust: where could proof, provenance, recovery, or user control become a feature?
- non-code: operational, educational, creative, hardware, document, community, or decision workflows;
- counterfactual: what would a team with ten times the ambition attempt?
- subtraction: what simplification creates more value than another feature?

Require each idea to state a mechanism. Reject names that could describe any product.

## Stress novelty and value

Cluster duplicates. For each survivor, specify:

- target user and moment;
- changed outcome;
- mechanism and required assets;
- why now;
- evidence or assumption;
- smallest falsifying experiment;
- dependencies and risks;
- value, effort, confidence, and strategic leverage.

Include a portfolio: a few near-term wins, a few compounding platform moves, and one or two bold bets.

## Converge

Rank by expected value, not excitement alone. Prefer ideas that:

- solve repeated or severe jobs;
- reuse existing strengths;
- create learning or proprietary data;
- improve several projects;
- make trust visible;
- remain valuable if a particular model or vendor changes.

## Output

Return:

1. opportunity map by theme;
2. top candidates with concise rationales;
3. discarded generic ideas and why;
4. recommended experiments;
5. unknowns requiring user or market evidence.
