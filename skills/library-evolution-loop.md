# Evolve the Library from Real Work

Treat the Library as a governed learning system, not a prompt collection.

## Capture candidates

After real work, look for:

- a successful non-obvious sequence likely to recur;
- a failure pattern and reliable diagnostic;
- a domain constraint agents repeatedly miss;
- a deterministic helper repeatedly recreated;
- a user preference confirmed across tasks;
- a standard, provider, or tool change that invalidates guidance;
- a skill that triggered badly, conflicted, or added no value.

Do not capture one-off facts, secrets, transient paths, generic advice, or unvalidated guesses.

## Propose

For every candidate include:

- triggering situations;
- smallest reusable procedure;
- expected output or evidence;
- provenance and source date;
- trust level;
- dependencies and conflicts;
- representative eval cases;
- freshness condition or review date.

Search the Library first. Revise or annotate an existing asset when it already owns the job. Keep proposals as drafts until a human publishes them.

## Evaluate

Use actual task artifacts where possible. Compare the candidate against baseline behavior. Check trigger precision, incremental task success, safety, context cost, and composition. Convert repeated instructions into scripts or templates when deterministic execution is more reliable.

## Maintain

On a regular cadence:

- inspect stale sources and changed tools;
- sample usage and outcomes without treating popularity as quality;
- merge duplicates;
- split overloaded assets;
- deprecate obsolete versions with a successor;
- preserve published snapshots and decision history;
- identify capability gaps from failed or slow tasks.

## Output

Maintain a curation queue with candidate, evidence, owner, status, next evaluation, and review date. The Library should become smaller and stronger when pruning is warranted.
